Nicholas Martellaro: a journy

It took me a long time to figure out what I wanted to do with my life. I went to a number of diffent schools in my life. I went to Linn State Technical College, The University of Missour, and Pennsylvania State University. At first I wanted to work with computers, and I persued an education in computer networking. Very close to ending my education, I decided that I wanted a change. I decided to study Art History and Archaeology, while I love the field I was not able to continue my education further.

For years after completing my education, I hunted for jobs within the field of Art History and Archaeology, but to no evail. I worked many customer service jobs as a grocery store department manager, manager of a pizza resturant, and customer care represtentative at a home security company. I found none of the work fulfilling, and needed something new. At a wedding for a friend, I ran into another friend from the past. He had a similar experiance to mine, but he took a certificate program for Geospatial Information Systems. He became a map maker and spoke highly of the work. It sounded appealing and I took this route for myself.

After completing my course work and getting a post-baccalaureate certificate, I eventually recieved my first position as a GIS technician. after a year in the field I was laid off due to contract work not coming in fast enough for the staffing levels that the company had. While getting drinks and drowning our sorrows with some co-workers, I found that one of my co-workers, who has been in the field for 20 years, has been laid off 6 times. I felt that I need better job security and have always liked programing. While in highschool, college and in my certificate program, I study some programing and enjoyed it each time.
